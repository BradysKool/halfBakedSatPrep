Inscribed Angles

An inscribed angle in a circle is an angle whose vertex is on the circle itself and whose sides each intersect the circle in exactly one other point. A key theorem states:

The measure of an inscribed angle is half the measure of its intercepted arc.

In other words, if an inscribed angle intercepts an arc with a measure of \( \theta \) degrees, the angle itself measures \( \frac{\theta}{2} \) degrees.
